Mauritius VAT Update: New Rs 3 Million Threshold from October 2025 — How Posterita POS Keeps You Compliant

Nina Brilane · September 13, 2025

The Mauritius Revenue Authority has announced a significant change in VAT compliance rules. Starting October 1, 2025, all businesses with yearly turnover of Rs 3 million or more in taxable supplies must register for VAT. This represents the first time many small and medium-sized businesses will operate under VAT obligations.

Key Announcement: New VAT Rule

"Any person whose annual taxable turnover exceeds or is likely to exceed Rs 3 million is required to be registered for VAT." — MRA VAT FAQs

Registration requirements:

  • Charge VAT on taxable goods and services
  • Issue VAT invoices
  • File monthly or quarterly returns
  • Maintain proper, auditable sales records
  • Register via MRA's Taxpayer Portal (TAP)

Risks of Manual Systems

Handwritten receipts or spreadsheet-based tracking expose businesses to:

  • VAT miscalculations or underreporting
  • Filing delays and errors
  • Difficulty tracking taxable turnover
  • MRA non-compliance fines
