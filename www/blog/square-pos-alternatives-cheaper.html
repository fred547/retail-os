Top 5 Square POS Alternatives That Are Actually Cheaper

Square POS has built a solid reputation as an accessible point-of-sale solution for small businesses. However, with transaction fees ranging from 2.6% to 3.5% and premium features locked behind subscription tiers, many business owners are actively seeking Square POS alternatives that deliver better value without sacrificing functionality.

The good news? There are excellent options available that can reduce your operating costs while potentially offering superior features tailored to your specific business needs. Whether you're running a retail store, restaurant, or service-based business, this guide will help you discover cost-effective POS alternatives that deserve your consideration.

Why Businesses Are Looking for Square POS Alternatives

Before diving into specific alternatives, it's important to understand why so many entrepreneurs are exploring other options. While Square offers convenience and ease of setup, the cumulative costs can become significant over time. Business owners cite several common reasons for switching:

  • High transaction fees that eat into profit margins
  • Limited customization for industry-specific needs
  • Expensive hardware requirements
  • Restricted reporting and analytics capabilities
  • Lack of advanced inventory management features
  • Poor customer support responsiveness

The search for affordable Square alternatives often reveals that many competing platforms offer more transparent pricing models and feature-rich solutions at comparable or lower price points.

If you already use Shopify for online sales, integrating Shopify POS creates seamless inventory synchronization and unified reporting. This eliminates duplicate fees and simplifies backend operations—a significant advantage over using Square alongside Shopify.

Why it's cheaper: Consolidation equals savings. Running both a Shopify store and Square separately means paying multiple sets of fees and managing duplicate systems. With Shopify POS, you get unified transactions at their standard rates without redundant monthly charges.

How to Choose the Right Square POS Alternative for Your Business

Evaluate Your Industry Requirements

Different businesses have different needs. A hair salon requires appointment scheduling; a restaurant needs kitchen management; a retail store needs inventory tracking. Choose a Square alternative built for your specific industry rather than trying to force a general-purpose system to work.

Calculate Total Cost of Ownership

Don't just compare monthly fees. Factor in transaction percentages, hardware costs, required integrations, and training time. The cheapest upfront option may cost more when you account for all factors. Create a spreadsheet projecting 12-month costs based on your expected transaction volume.

Test Before Committing

Most POS providers offer free trials. Use them. Test the system with your actual workflow, not just in ideal scenarios. Check how intuitive the interface is for your team and whether customer support responds helpfully when you have questions.

Consider Integration Needs

Verify that your chosen platform integrates smoothly with your accounting software, email marketing tools, loyalty programs, and any other business systems. Poor integrations lead to manual data entry, errors, and wasted time—negating cost savings.

"Switching POS systems might seem risky, but the long-term financial benefits and operational improvements often justify the migration effort. Most modern POS providers offer comprehensive onboarding support to make transitions smooth."

Making the Switch: Migration Tips

Concerned about switching from Square? Here's what to expect:

  • Data migration: Most platforms offer tools to import customer data, transaction history, and inventory from your existing system
  • Hardware compatibility: Check whether you need new hardware or can repurpose existing devices
  • Training: Allocate time for staff training—plan for 1–2 weeks of parallel operation while your team adjusts
  • Timing: Schedule migrations during slow business periods to minimize disruption
  • Payment processing: Coordinate with your new provider to ensure payment processing activates before you deactivate Square

Conclusion: Finding Your Ideal POS Solution
